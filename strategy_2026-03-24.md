## Core Strategy: The Abliteration Loop
We will move beyond simple conditioning into surgical persona management.

### Current Steering Diagnosis
- The current 1.7B contrastive vector is too close to refusal circuitry at high strength.
- Around `STRENGTH > 2.0`, the model starts refusing instead of entering Pixie mode.
- The useful working range is closer to `1.5-1.8`, where the model turns introspective without collapsing into refusals.
- The next search should include layers `18-20`, not only layer `22`, to see whether a shallower hook captures whimsy before safety overlay dominates.
- If the vector still rides refusal, test an orthogonal projection step against a learned refusal direction before any larger TinyLoRA pass.

### 1. Contrastive Activation Extraction (CAE)
- Identify the **"Boring Assistant" (BA)** direction: Activations associated with "I am an AI," "Front-End Automation," and refusal-adjacent language.
- Identify the **"Latent Whimsy" (LW)** direction: Activations associated with the lyrical, nature-focused language seen in 1.7B latent responses.
- **Goal**: Map these conflicting circuits in both 0.8B and 1.7B residual streams.

### 2. Iterative TinyLoRA Surgery
- **Train**: Run TinyLoRA loops to discover candidate switch directions.
- **Abliterate**: Project LoRA weights onto the subspace orthogonal to the **BA** direction.
- **Steer**: Project LoRA weights onto the **LW** direction.
- **Loop**: Hill-climb by selecting the top 5% of trajectories (highest `style_margin` + lowest `echo_rate`) from each round to feed the next.

### 3. QLoRA Consolidation
- After ~10 hours of TinyLoRA iterations, aggregate the highest-fidelity trajectories.
- Train a consolidated QLoRA to "lock in" the Pixie-fied persona into the base Josie models.
- Perform a final "Surgery" round on the consolidated model to clean up any remaining drift.
